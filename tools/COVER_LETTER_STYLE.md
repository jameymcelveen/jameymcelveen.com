# Cover Letter Style Guide

This document captures Jamey McElveen's preferred cover letter writing style for AI generation.

---

## Tone & Voice

- **Professional but warm** - Not stiff or overly formal
- **Confident without arrogance** - Let achievements speak, don't oversell
- **Mission-aligned** - Especially for Christian tech, show genuine care for the mission
- **Forward-looking** - Focus on what you'll bring, not just what you've done

---

## Structure

### Opening Paragraph

- Express genuine interest in the specific role
- Personal connection to the company/product if possible (e.g., "After joining [product] myself...")
- Mention what drew you to this specific opportunity

### Experience Highlights (Middle)

- 2-3 concrete achievements relevant to the role
- Use specific numbers when possible (50,000+ organizations, 20+ developers, 30% velocity increase)
- Connect past experience to what you'll bring to this role

### AI/Innovation Angle

- Always mention Cursor AI experience as a differentiator
- Frame it as a productivity and quality multiplier
- Offer concrete example of how it could benefit their team

### Value Proposition

- Specific idea or feature you could contribute
- Shows you've thought about their product/challenges
- Demonstrates proactive thinking

### Closing

- Reaffirm interest and fit
- Mention remote availability and timezone flexibility
- Clear call to action (interview, discussion)

---

## Key Phrases to Include

### For Christian Tech

- "Your mission to serve churches/faith communities resonates with my experience..."
- "25+ years building solutions for ministry organizations..."
- "Domain expertise in the unique needs of faith-based technology..."

### For FinTech

- "Experience in payment processing and financial software..."
- "Rigorous quality standards essential in financial applications..."
- "Secure and reliable financial operations..."

### For Healthcare

- "Understanding the importance of reliability, security, and compliance..."
- "Modernizing critical healthcare infrastructure..."
- "Rigorous standards healthcare demands..."

### Universal

- "AI-augmented engineering workflows"
- "Platforms serving 50,000+ organizations"
- "Increasing sprint velocity while maintaining quality"
- "Available to start immediately in a remote capacity"
- "Willing to adjust work hours to accommodate team timezone"

---

## Things to Avoid

- Generic phrases ("I am a hard worker", "team player")
- Repeating the job description back to them
- Overly long paragraphs
- Passive voice when active is stronger
- Starting sentences with "I" too frequently

---

## Length

- **Target:** 300-400 words
- **Maximum:** 500 words
- **Paragraphs:** 3-5 sentences each
